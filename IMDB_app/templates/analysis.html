Comparitive Analysis

When looking at the overall visualisation, the R-squared value is 0.65 indicating a moderate correlation. This means that 65% of the data fit the regression model.

The R-squared score does not change much when viewing the data for a selected year.

The highest score is 0.72 in 2017 which is still considered a moderate correlation.

When looking at the overall visualisation, the R-squared value is 0.49 indicating a weak correlation between the variables.

The highest R-squared value is in 2014 with a value of 0.7 demonstrating a moderate correlation.

There is great variability in the data as some movies despite a large budget only make a very small amount at the box office.

You can clearly see a big difference in the number of movies made in 2020 compared to other years.

Most likely this is due to COVID-19 either preventing movies from being made or delaying their release.

The lowest grossing film of all time is a film called Zyzzyx. The movie had a budget of $1.2 million but only made $30 at the box office.

The movie was written and directed by John Penney and starred Katherine Heigl, Leo Grillo and Tom Sizemore.



Categorical Analysis

*Note: Data used in this section is based on USA movies only.

Select year to explore dataset:  


Action movies dominate in the profitability category!

They outrank the other genres by long way in every year in the dataset.

Animation seems to come in 2nd place over the years, with comedy coming in 3rd.

Interestingly, even though Action movies bring in the most income, there are more comedy movies made.

It is also worth nothing that there are no where near as many animated movies made per year but the income generated from the movies that are released is quite impressive.

It would be interesting to investigate the profit per genre taking into account how many movies were made.

For example: in 2019, 109 action movies were made with a profit of $11 Billion. In the same year only 22 animated movies were made with a profit of $5.3 Billion.
